Internal manual checks

Once the claimant has registered, the PIP Service router will automatically run rules based on what the claimant has entered.

If the rules to retain it in the new service are met and the service isn't at capacity it will continue through the thin slice.

A Case Worker will use the registration data to manually complete the eligibility checks using other systems like Customer Information System (CIS) / Searchlight. They will record the results of the eligibility checks on the new service.

Case Workers will have access to Agent Led Process' and guidance to support them with eligibility and other checks.

Eligibility checks include age, accommodation, overlapping benefits, residence and presence. Other assurance checks include checking data matches other systems.

If the answers to the eligibility question make it complicated, then it will be routed back to PIPCS for Business as Usual.

If the claimant doesn't meet the eligibility rules, the Case Worker will disallow them.

Some checks happen before it's sent for a Health Assessment and some happen after. Some checks will need to be repeated throughout the journey.

Once the Health Information Gather has been completed by the claimant and there are no further tasks, the case is referred to the Health Assessment Service (HAS).
